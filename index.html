Tutorial

Learn the fundamentals.

Psychrometrics is the study of the thermodynamic properties of moist air. Master these seven properties and you can analyze any HVAC process.

What is Psychrometrics?

The science of moist air — a binary mixture of dry air and water vapor at atmospheric pressure.

Atmospheric air is never completely dry. It contains varying amounts of water vapor — typically 0.005 to 0.030 kg per kg of dry air. Even though this moisture seems small, it carries enormous amounts of latent heat and drives weather, comfort, and HVAC design.

The psychrometric chart is a graphical representation of these properties at a fixed pressure (usually sea level, 101.325 kPa). Once you can read it, you can solve complex air-conditioning problems visually — finding energy costs, condensation points, and required equipment capacities in seconds.

Patm = Pda + Pw   (Dalton's Law)

The Seven Properties

Each property is interrelated — given any two, the rest are determined.

Reading the Chart

The psychrometric chart packs seven properties onto a single 2D plot. Here's how to decode it.

1
Horizontal axis — Dry-bulb temperature
The "regular" air temperature measured by a thermometer. Increases left to right.
2
Vertical axis — Humidity ratio
Mass of water vapor per kg of dry air. Increases bottom to top.
3
Curved boundary — Saturation line
The upper-left curve is 100% RH. All possible air states lie below it.
4
Diagonal lines — Wet-bulb temperature
Lines sloping down-right represent constant wet-bulb temperature.
5
Steep diagonals — Enthalpy (Total Heat)
Enthalpy lines run almost parallel to wet-bulb lines but are slightly steeper. They are typically labeled on the upper-left or outer scale. To read enthalpy, locate your state point and trace parallel to the enthalpy lines back to the enthalpy scale.
